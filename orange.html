Orange Fruit


Botanical Background and Origin The orange, specifically the sweet orange (Citrus × sinensis), is a hybrid between the pomelo (Citrus maxima) and the mandarin (Citrus reticulata) with the maternal line from pomelo. It originated in a region covering Southern China, Northeast India, and Myanmar, with the earliest mentions in Chinese literature dating back to 314 BC. Orange trees are small, evergreen, flowering trees that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers.
